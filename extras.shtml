Fixture Modifier

Fixture Modifiers are anything that can be attached to a fixture to enhance, change, or control the light given off by it.

Gels and Gel Frames

Many color gels, some in gel frames

A gel is a colored transparent plastic like material used to change the color of the light given off by a conventional fixture. Gels typically come in 3x3 feet sheets and can be cut to fit your desired fixture. To use a gel you must place it in a gel frame that is suited for the fixture. Strip lights used for cycs typically have diffrent gels in order to mix the colors. Gels can be very useful when it comes to designing.

Gobo

metal gobo in a gobo holder

Gobos are metal or glass disks that are cut out to form a shapes, images, or text. A gobo mus be placed in a gobo holder. A gobo holder can only be inserted into an ellipsoidal towards the middle or gobos are built into some movers. There are companies that will create a custom gobo of your design.

Barn Door

typical Barn Door

A barn door is something to put on the front of fresnel to control where the light is thrown. There are flaps on hinges which appear like doors of a barn that can be bent out to your liking. Because of the wash nature of fresnels, barn doors can be very useful in containing light from one. A barn door attaches to the same place the gel frame would go.

Top Hat

typical Top Hat

Top hats shield the light from the audiences eyes. Even if the focus is on stage, the lense from a fixture is visible to the audience can be distracting and looks sloppy. Also, if a light is near the proscenium or something else that needs to be dark, a top hat can prevent spill over light from hitting it. Top hats are attached using the same place a gel frame would go.

Utility

Data Supply

5 pin DMX cable

To send data, a specialized 5 pin XLR cable is required. DMX is the standard format the data is sent and can be interchanged with the term 'data'. Many intelligent fixtures will be 3 pin, in which case an adapter from 5 pin to 3 pin is perfectly fine to use. Be sure to use DMX lighting 3 pin and not sound XLR, as this may cause problems. A Optical Isolator or Iso for short is a device that can be used as a hub of DMX. There is an in which comes from the board and multiple outs tha can be used to send to fixtures in different directions. Using a Y-splitter will cause problems in data transferring. Fixtures will have a data in as well as data out which can be used for daisy chaining one fixture to another. On the last fixture it is not required but is recommended to have a terminator on the data out to prevent uncontrollable flashes by the other fixtures.

Power Supply

stage pin extension cord
powercon ends

A strip of of power plugs is referred to as raceways. The standard power plug used is stage pin and virtually all raceways and conventional fixtures use. Extension cords in stage pin are available, but any extensions cords used should be as short as possible to reduce resistance. Adapters with stagepin and Edison are also prevalent. Common amongst intelligent fixtures is a power port called powercon, and comes with a powercon to Edison adapter. Many times there will be a powercon power out port on a fixture in oder to achieve a daisy chain of power.

Gaff Tape

roll of black gaff tape

Gaffers tape common referred to as Gaff tape can be used for an assortment of uses. Typically in black, it usually does not leave a sticky residue to the surface applied after removal. A common use is to tape down exposed cables to the floor in a neat fashion. It can also be used to tape together a gel frame. A good habit is to put a piece of tap an all of your intelligent fixtures and wright the address for easy reference. On boards with several faders, a technique used to to place tape below all faders with a word or two describing what or where it controls.
